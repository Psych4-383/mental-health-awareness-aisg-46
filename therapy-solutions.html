Therapy Solutions

Therapies (Solutions)- Many people diagnosed with mental illness achieve strength and recovery through participating in individual or group treatments. There are many different types of treatments. There is no treatment that works for everyone, individuals can choose the treatment that works best.


Psychotherapy- Psychotherapy is the remedial treatment for mental illnesses provided by a trained mental health professional. Psychotherapy helps in exploring thoughts, feelings, behaviors, and seeks to improve an individual’s well-being.


Medication- Medication does not outright cure mental illness. However, it may help with the management of symptoms. Medication paired with psychotherapy is the most effective way to promote recovery.

Support Group- A support group is a group meeting where members guide each other towards the shared goal of recovery. Support groups are often comprised of non-professionals, but people that have suffered from similar experiences.


Hospitalization- In a minority of cases, hospitalization may be necessary so that an individual can be closely and professionally monitored, accurately diagnosed or have medications adjusted when his or her mental illness temporarily worsens.


Complementary & Alternative Medicine- Complementary & Alternative Medicine, or CAM, refers to treatment and practices that are not typically associated with standard care. CAM may be used in place of or addition to standard health practices.


Peer Support- Peer Support refers to receiving help from individuals who have suffered from similar experiences.
