Effects of the Pandemic

Pandemics are stressful situations for people and communities. Fear and anxiety with a lack of knowledge about the disease often leads to social stigma. COVID-19 pandemic altered the way of living, making a drastic change in the lives of the people. Schools and universities closed, exams and events got postponed, the usual health information services were limited, socializing with friends and a wider family was highly discouraged and, in some places, punishable. The sense of uncertainty and helplessness consumed them beyond control. People lost their jobs, friends, and family members. Living in these circumstances was tough for these people and had a significant impact on mental health.

Most of the people feared contracting COVID-19 to themselves or their relatives. Many children and parents indicated COVID-19 being the main factor affecting their emotions. Children were anxious about school closures, interrupted access to basic services and their families’ economic hardships due to COVID-19 containment measures. Many parents reported changes in how their children spend their days in the community since the start of the pandemic. Many noticed changes in their relationships with their children, including children’s aggressive behavior, and stress and pressure on both children and parents.

Children also identified family poverty and food insecurity as a chief concern. For children in poor or displaced families, job losses placed them at grave risk of food insecurity resulting in violations of their rights and protections. This in turn contributes to their increased sense of helplessness and stress too. Bereavement, isolation, loss of income and fear triggered many people’s mental health conditions. Many people faced depression, anxiety, and insomnia during these tough times.
